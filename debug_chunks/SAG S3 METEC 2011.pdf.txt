Plenary Session 7 th ECCC Session 1 1 Düsseldorf, 27 June – 1 July 2011 The New Bloom Caster S3 at Saar- stahl G. Ney, K. Harste, K.-J. Richter, U. Reinstädtler, S. Wagner, E. Korte, R. Thome, S. Feldhaus, A. N. Grundy ABSTRACT Saarstahl is one of the world’s leading suppliers for long products of unalloyed and alloyed quality and high-grade steels. Traditionally Saarstahl AG is at the forefront of casting technologies for products with the highest demands on quality. In order to increase this technological lead and to achieve improvements both with respect to the qual- ity of the products as well as to increase the flexibility and capacity of the production an investment was made in a new 5-strand bloom caster, termed S3. The S3 is equipped to cast 2402 mm2 and 265x340 mm2 blooms with lengths of 3.0 to 12.5 m. 25 months after awarding the contract for building the new caster S3 the first heat was cast in September 2009. The following paper outlines the main features of the caster and presents metallurgical results. 1. INTRODUCTION Saarstahl’s LD steel plant in Völklingen was estab- lished in the early 1980s. TECHNOLOGY The continuous caster S3 is a 5 strand bow type continuous caster with curved moulds. The concep- Plenary Session 7 th ECCC Session 1 2 Düsseldorf, 27 June – 1 July 2011 tual design of the caster S3 is to cast blooms (For- mats 2402 and 265x340 mm2) with the option of me- chanical soft reduction (MSR). The caster radius is 10.5 m. The caster and discharge area are designed for maximal casting speeds of 1.25 m/min (Format 240 mm2) and 0.9 m/min (Format 265x340 mm2). The metallurgical length of the caster (distance from me- niscus to begin torch cutting) is 28.5 m, the mould length is 800 mm. The secondary cooling

--- CHUNK 4 ---
length is ~4.0 m and consists of three independent cooling zones. Zone 1 is water-only; zones 2 and 3 are air- mist cooling. The length of the withdrawal straighten- ing and MSR unit is ~8.4 m. Figure 1 shows photographic views of the machine head, moulds and the tundish, the strand guiding and roller table area with sample torch cutting machines. Figure 2: Photographic views of the caster S3 Caster Head In order to enable sequence casting, the caster S3 has an electrically driven ladle turret with two inde- pendently movable rotating arms. The ladle capacity is 170 t. The steel stream from the ladle is protected by a sealed box flushed with inert gas that is placed over the pouring point of the tundish. The tundish (delta-type) with a nominal capacity of 21 t is preheated in 3 phases during 3 h up to a tem- perature of 1200 °C. Steel is teemed into the mould through mono-block submerged entry nozzles (SEN). The flow is regulated by hydraulically actuated mono- block stopper rods. The mould level is controlled by a radioactive measuring system (Co60 source). Powder feeding -only granulated powder is used- is automatic via a metering unit. For

--- CHUNK 5 ---
this purpose each strand is equipped with a container that rests on a load cell. A defined amount of powder is fed into a small hopper and gravimetrically transported into the mould via a squeeze valve. Both the amount of pow- der and the cycle time for powder addition can be variably chosen. The mould is equipped with a cartridge system that allows changing the mould tube together with the water jacket without dismounting the mould electro- magnetic stirrer (M-EMS) and the mould frame (Fig- ure 3). The spray ring with the water nozzle ar- rangement for the secondary cooling zone 1 (so called “water-box”) is attached to the bottom of the mould frame. The water-box encloses the foot roll frame that comprises 2 rows with 4 rolls each. To change the casting format (from 240x240 to 265x340 mm2 or vice versa) both the mould cartridge and the water-box needs to be changed. Figure 3: Mould assembly The caster S3 is equipped with a hydraulic resonance oscillator (Figure 4). The mould frame is placed on two oscillation units that are mounted on a base frame. Actuation is by two hydraulic cylinders that are synchronized relative to each other in order

Plenary Session 7 th ECCC Session 1 4 Düsseldorf, 27 June – 1 July 2011 Withdrawal Straightening and MSR Unit / Models The withdrawal straightening / mechanical soft reduc- tion unit consists of 8 MSR segments per strand. Each MSR segment consists of one lower roll idle roll with a fixed position and one driven upper roll that can be vertically actu-ated by a hydraulic cylinder. Figure 6 compares the withdrawal straightening unit of the S3 with the old S1. The casting of steel grades that are sensitive to transversal surface straightening cracks is made possible by the smooth transition from the casting radius to the horizontal over 4 straightening points. This concept also reduces the straightening strain at the solidification front thereby reducing the danger of forming internal cracks. Figure 6: Schematic comparison of the withdrawal straightening units of the S3 and the S1 The hydraulics of the MSR segments allows each roll to be pressed

--- CHUNK 9 ---
onto the strand with a maximum force of 1400 kN thereby reducing the thickness of the strand. The reduction of the strand can be both by so called mechanical soft reduction and mechanical hard reduction. The former method involves reducing the strand while the core is still liquid or mushy the latter method involves reducing the strand immedi- ately after the point of final solidification. The upper roll of each MSR segment is pressed onto the strand either in force control mode or position control mode. The format and steel grade dependent force- or posi- tion set points for each roll are taken from static ta- bles or are dynamically calculated during casting. To monitor and regulate the metallurgical working range during MSR the caster is coupled to a dynamic solidification model (DSC, Dynamic Solidification Control). The model calculates the solidification and the temperature distribution of all strands based on the actual casting parameters and autonomously corrects these should a deviation occur between defined set-points and the calculated value of metal- lurgically relevant values such as straightening tem- perature or point of final solidification. The MSR set- points are stored in format and steel grade depend- ent tables. Depending on

--- CHUNK 10 ---
the chosen regulation method these set points can be adhered to by using the model to adjust the secondary cooling or the cast- ing speed. It is thereby possible to reduce strand and process specific fluctuations of the MSR working range and increase the reproducibility of the quality values when applying the MSR technology. Roller Table Area The discharge of the blooms is fully automatic. Mate- rial tracking occurs via light barriers and IR sensors. After exiting the withdrawal straightening unit torch cutters cut the strands into blooms with lengths rang- ing from 3.0 to 12.5 m for both casting formats. The roller table area (Figure 7) is divided into 8 groups comprising 5 or 6 driven rolls each. The steel tape dummy bars are raised on top of the roller table by a hydraulic lifting unit after disconnection from the hot strand in the region of roll groups 3 and 4. At the end of roll group 4 the blooms are stopped by re- tractable end-stops to be marked with heat-, strand-, and bloom numbers. The caster disposes of two re- volver head bloom marking machines that are mounted on a bridge. During normal casting, marking machine no. 1

--- CHUNK 11 ---
marks strands 1 to 3, marking ma- chine no. 2 marks strands 4 and 5. Should one of the marking machines be defective all strands can be marked with a single marking machine. After mark- ing, samples can be cut for example for sulphur prints. For this purpose as a sample torch cutter is installed on every strand. Immediately after the mark- ing machine and sample cutters in the area of roll group 5 a cross transfer is installed that enables the crop ends to be removed during casting. At roll group 6 one pneumatically actuated deburrer is installed for each strand. A down-holder pushes the bloom head or tail respectively down onto the roller table while a horizontally rotating disc with hardened steel knives is pressed against the bloom from the bottom. The burrs that remain on the head and tail of the blooms after torch cutting and sample cutting are thereby removed so that no further conditioning of the blooms is required before rolling. Turn-Over Cooling Bed The roll groups no. 7 and 8 of the roller table trans- port the blooms to the end-stops of the roller table. Retractable end-stops are installed between roll groups no. 7 and 8 in order to allow double banking of the cross transfer area when short bloom lengths are cut. The blooms are pushed off the roller table by a cross transfer. The so called position pusher pushes the packets of blooms to the foot of the ~5 m high elevator. From there the blooms can either be removed from the caster by a crane to be placed under isolation hoods for slow cooling and H2 deple- tion or can be transported to the turn-over cooling bed depending on grade specific cooling instructions. The elevator transports the blooms onto the turn-over cooling bed that is 22 m long and 13.25 m wide. It is hydraulically driven and consists of two longitudinally separated fields that can be synchronically operated when the bloom length is greater than 6 m. In order to achieve even cooling the blooms are lifted by a mobile

--- CHUNK 13 ---
rake and placed onto the next notch of the stationary rake. Thereby the blooms are rotated by 90°. At the end of the turn-over cooling bed the blooms are pushed together in packets of varying size (up to 4 blooms per pack) and are loaded onto railway wag- ons and transported to the rolling mill. 3. Surface Quality of As-Cast Strands The surface quality of the as-cast blooms and sand blasted blooms was extensively inspected under normal light and also under LED light in the dry mag- netic flux facility (Mecana facility). The initial extent of inspection of 100% was markedly reduced with time in view of the good surface quality of the different steel grades. The established casting parameters resulted in a very good as cast surface quality with respect to regularity of oscillation marks. The fraction of blooms showing transversal corner cracks could be significantly re- duced for Nb, V,Ti and N micro-alloyed grades. Steel Cleanness The primary aim was to keep the macro- and micro steel cleanness of the cast strands at least

at the same good level achieved on the caster S1. The macro cleanness was investigated both by blue shortness analysis and by ultrasonic inspection of forged samples and compared to the quality level of corresponding steel grades cast on the S1. During the period of one year a total of 185 heats were ana- lysed using blue shortness analysis and 88 further heats were analysed by ultrasonic inspection. Even at the significantly higher casting speeds of the caster S3 very good steel cleanness is achieved compara- ble to the material obtained from the S1. The same results were confirmed for the extensive micro steel cleanness investigations. It has further been confirmed by systematic sampling and comparison of steel cleanness results that no difference exists between the 5 strands. In addition the rolled products were subjected to sur- face crack and macroscopic cleanness analysis using ultrasonic inspection. The achieved results for the single steel grades and dimension of rolled products were compared to the good results from the caster S1 and were found to be on the same good level. Outlook Over all it can be said that after the successful com- missioning of the billet caster S0 in the year 2004 Saarstahl AG now after the commissioning of the caster S3 disposes of both a billet- as well as a bloom caster that are on the most recent level of continuous casting technology. This allows the cast- ing of steel grades with the highest quality demands. On the caster S0 the MSR-technology is already employed to reduce the centre segregation (for ex- ample for bearing steels). The future development on the S3, aside the steel grade and format specific optimization of the casting process, is focussed on improving the internal quality of the as cast blooms. Here the focus is on utilizing and optimizing the MSR technology to reduce

the centre segregation and centre porosity for demanding steel grades for forging and automobile applications. The aim is the production of bar- and semi-finished material that is as homogeneous as possible and fulfils customer requirements even for largest dimen- sions with respect to centre segregation, centre soundness and deformation degree. At the same time by utilizing material cast using the MSR technol- ogy an improvement with respect to cost and produc- tivity is aimed for, as cost intensive diffusion anneal- ing and further processing steps can be omitted. A further focus is the establishment of the larger for- mat together with the customers.
