Emissivity compensation algorithms in double-band pyrometry H. Madura *, T. Piatkowski Institute of Optoelectronics, Military University of Technology, 2 Kaliskiego Street, 00-908 Warsaw, Poland Available online 16 April 2004 Abstract Recently, double-band pyrometers have been more frequently used for remote temperature measurements. A lot of modiﬁcations of double-band method are known. These methods minimise temperature measure errors for determined types of materials. New compensation method of temperature measurement devoted for metal objects with minimum error has been proposed. Computer simulations of the method are carried out for Al, Fe, Cr, Cu, and for two kinds of bricks.  2004 Elsevier B.V. All rights reserved. Keywords: Temperature measurement; Double-band pyrometry; Emissivity 1. Introduction Pyrometers used as instruments for remote temperature measurement are applied in industry, science and medicine. Due to their availability and relatively low prices, the number of their users still increases. Manufacturers equip the new pyrome- ters with optical and detection systems as well as with microprocessor systems that reduce mea- surement errors due to application of new mea- surement methods and special software [1]. The main source of errors in pyrometric measurements is a value of emissivity of the investigated object. Many users do not know this value

or estimate it with a signiﬁcant error. For these cases manufac- turers suggest application of double-band pyrom- eters [1]. For grey bodies, these pyrometers do not introduce an error resulting from physical phe- nomena but many physical objects are not grey. They have emissivity dependent on wavelength of the emitted radiation. Solution of the problem is possible due to increase in a number of optical measuring bands (but a pyrometer price increases) or due to modiﬁcation of temperature measure- ment method for dual-band pyrometers. Limita- tion of the latter solution is adaptation of the pyrometers for determined class of objects. 2. Power function modiﬁcation of ratio method for double-band pyrometers During the works on the project of development of construction and performance of a double-band pyrometer, all methods of temperature measure- ments in two spectral bands were analysed. The analysis included also typical measurements and the used materials. It appeared that the centres manufacturing and processing metals as well as metalworking ones are especially interested in double-band pyrometers. Thus, the temperature measuring method should be modiﬁed in such a way to ensure compensation of emissivity inﬂuence for the whole class of materials, i.e., metals. The metal’s emissivity is described by the Hagen– Rubens law. For metals in long wavelength radi- ation range, emissivity is a function of superﬁcial resistance and frequency of electromagnetic oscil- lations. It takes the form: eðkÞ ¼ 2
