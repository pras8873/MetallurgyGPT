CC technology Session 5 1 Düsseldorf, 27 June – 1 July 2011 Corresponding author: Kyung Shik Oh, POSCO, P.O.Box 36, Pohang, Korea, +82-54-220-4244(tel), +82-54-220-6832(fax), goldsteel@posco.com Introduction Internal quality of a CC bloom is very important for both manufacturing process and final service properties of high carbon steel. Breakage occurs during drawing and stranding process due to center segregation[1] in case of tire cord steel and it reduces productivity of tire cord wire. For bearing steel, center segregation causes shortage of fatigue life of ball or roller bearings[2]. Strict demand on internal quality of high grade steels such as a tire cord, bearing, and cable wire steel, etc. has been being bigger and bigger continually together with demand on development of higher performance steel. In case of a large bloom caster without any quality improving equipments, center segregation of high carbon steel is preferable to occur greatly comparing with conventional one of bloom thickness size less than 300mm in spite of better productivity and surface quality. Therefore soft reduction units of 12m-long-reduction zone as well as mold and final EMS have been installed at #2 bloom caster of 400x500mm2 section size in Pohang Works of POSCO in order to reduce center

segregation of the large bloom. Applying soft reduction technology, reduction zone, reduction amount, and reduction rate should be optimized to meet excellent soundness of the bloom. In case of the large bloom, micro segregation at center region should be considered to decide solidus line during casting in order to find optimum reduction zone and effective reduction amount is thought to be important in view point of finding out optimum reduction amount. In this study heat transfer analysis has been carried out and solidus temperature was derived after micro segregation of solute elements was calculated with considering dendrite structure. Base on the above results soft reduction test was carried out in the derived reduction zone, which was modified afterwards from investigation of the bloom specimens. In order to find out optimum reduction amount, effective reduction amount was estimated from deformation analysis at the reduction roll during casting when soft reduction applied. Soft reduction test was carried out with various reduction amounts considering effective reduction amount. Convex type roll was also used to enhance effective reduction amount and reduce reduction roll force. By aid of the optimum soft reduction condition internal soundness of the large bloom of high carbon steel was greatly

In this investigation, a bloom is assumed to undergo 3- dimensional deformation isothermally during soft reduction using a single roll. The upper and lower rolls measuring 450mm in diameter, rotate in opposite directions at a rate of 0.039 rad/sec (casting speed = 0.53mpm). The friction at the roll/bloom interface is assumed to obey the coulomb friction law with the factor of 0.4. ABAQUS, a commercial code for the finite element analysis of metal forming, is extensively used. The rolls are treated as rigid bodies while the bloom was treated as a rigid-plastic material. Only the half width of the bloom is included in the analysis because of its symmetry in deformation. To predict the deformation at narrow interface with approximately 20~40mm contact length, more than 9000 elements with fine meshes in a contact region are used. When the bloom is subjected to 4mm reduction by a roll, Fig. 4(a) presents the area variation of mushy zone by roll shape used. Summary In order to improve internal quality of the large high carbon bloom soft reduction condition has been reviewed in view point of reduction zone with consideration of micro-segregation and necessary reduction amount. The results are summarized as follows. 1) The crater end position found by the solidus temperature calculated from combined calculation of thermo-mechanical and segregation models was found to move far downwards compared with other results. 2) As reduction effectiveness was estimated to be 37.5% and 48.8% for 400x500mm2 bloom and 300x400mm2 bloom, respectively, much more reduction amount is necessary in case of 400x500mm2 bloom than 300x400mm2 considering more solidification shrinkage additionally. 3) Widthwise reduction effectiveness applied by a convex roll was found to be greatly higher than flat roll while thickness-wise one was found
