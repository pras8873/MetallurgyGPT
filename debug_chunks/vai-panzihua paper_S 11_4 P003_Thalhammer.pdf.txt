INTRODUCTION In September 2001 VAI received a contract for the delivery of a 6 – strand bloom caster for Panzhihua Iron & Steel Co. (Pangang) in P.R. China. The caster is designed for an annual capacity of 1.2 million tons of blooms to feed the existing rolling mill. The two bloom formats are 280x 325 and 280 x 380mm, as shown in Figure 1. Unique is that this plant is equipped with a DynaGap soft reduction package. To successfully handle the operation of the DynaGap soft reduction process a 3D-thermal model was developed by VAI, which calculates the solid fraction profile on-line and allows efficient dynamic soft reduction also during unstable casting conditions. VAI was chosen for its well-known and highly reliable casting technology and as a supplier with the know-how of operating a CC machine on a very high productivity and quality level. The high performance bloom caster completely replaces the existing ingot casting route for rail, welding wire, cord, reinforced bar and low/medium alloyed carbon steel grades. According

Figure 3: Optimized VAI segment design – segment 1 2.1 Bloom DynaGap Soft Reduction Technology [3] [4] With the ever-increasing range of products cast on bloom casters, the demands placed on plant operators and suppliers are also constantly increasing. Typical examples of steel grades that are rather difficult to cast are high-carbon steels, as used for example for tire cord, rail steel and ball bearing steels. Special emphases are placed on these products by the steel plants in order to minimize center segregation and porosity. Center segregation is caused by suction of highly segregated rest melt into the area of final solidification [1], due to the higher cooling rate and

--- CHUNK 4 ---
resulting higher reduction in volume in the center area of the strand in comparison to the strand shell. This rest melt originates partly from interdendritic zones and is therefore highly enriched with alloying elements, which will concentrate in the strand core. A suitable approach towards reducing center segregation during final solidification is to extend the area of equiaxed solidification. As a consequence, the segregations are more evenly distributed and so-called center bridging is prevented. For this purpose, special measures are taken, such as low superheat casting, electromagnetic stirring [3], or adding external nuclei. Another step towards improving the situation in terms of segregation, is to increase the cross-sectional reduction in the area of final solidification. This is achieved by greatly increasing the cooling rate at the strand surface (thermal soft reduction), or by reducing the strand cross section in the area of final solidification by means of rollers (mechanical soft reduction). The Pangang bloom caster is unique worldwide because it is equipped and automated with DynaGap soft reduction capability developed by VAI, see Figure 4. To get the best internal quality, it is necessary to permanently adjust the soft reduction area and the soft reduction amount to the following main

SUMMARY The final-acceptance certificate (FAC) for the Pangang bloom caster was already signed 3 months after start up. This was a result of the very good cooperation and excellent personnel from both companies, Pangang and VAI. The new bloom DynaGap soft reduction technology was a great step in the advanced VAI bloom casting technology to improve internal quality (segregation, porosity). This proven VAI technology will bring great benefits to today’s and the future’s high quality bloom market, especially to cover the increasing demands of the end users of the products. 5.
