HIGH INTERNAL QUALITY BILLET PRODUCTION WITH SRD – LONG CASTER SEGMENTS M. Riedler, D. Burzic, P. Pennerstorfer, A. Jungbauer – Primetals Technologies Austria GmbH, Austria Precise knowledge of the final solidification point and the associated soft reduction is needed to reliably produce billets for steel grades that require high internal quality. The new SRD segments from Primetals Technologies can precisely apply the soft reduction till the final solidification point. Each individual roll gap can be adjusted dynamically as a function of the steel grade, overheating, cooling or casting speed. Each roll transmits an individual force, which makes even higher thickness reduction rates possible, and reduces the segregation and porosity in the center of the strand. Additionally, thickness reduction of the billet or bloom also after final solidification is possible. This process is defined as Hard Reduction and can furthermore reduce the porosity of the cast billet and bloom. Technological packages like DynaPhase, Dynacs 3D and DynaGap SoftReduction 3D will further improve the internal billet quality with regard to center porosity and center segregation. Operational flexibility is increased by soft and hard reduction at all casting conditions like start of cast, stoppages or waiting for steel. Easy maintenance and complete testing,

KEYWORDS: BILLET – BLOOM – CONTINUOUS CASTING – INTERNAL QUALITY – SRD TECHNOLOGY – SOFT REDUCTION – HARD REDUCTION – SEGREGATION – POROSITY INTRODUCTION Market demand in the steel industry is changing very rapidly in the current economic environment, and cost- efficient production combined with high flexibility is absolutely essential to a profitable business. To meet these requirements, continuous improvements of end products, the production process, and production facilities are recommendable and essential [1]. The developing trend in the automotive industry goes towards smaller and lighter components coupled with the demand for components that can bear greater loads and stresses. This trend pushes steel producers to introduce special steels that can meet requirements of the automotive industry. This calls for flexible and well-designed production plants with reduced maintenance and longer equipment lifetime. This paper provides an overview about latest realized solutions for billet continuous casters on the example of a bow type caster, which is equipped with latest technological packages. DYNAMIC SOFT-REDUCTION TECHNOLOGY To realize dynamic soft reduction, it is necessary to control the pinch roll gaps

according to the actual solidification, taking into account current and past conditions. The shell growth defining the soft reduction area is influenced throughout its lifetime in the casting machine by many factors, from superheat in the mold to water amounts in the secondary cooling zones. Figure 2 shows the DynaGap 3D system which uses the actual computed parameters from the thermal tracking system Dynacs 3D to calculate the correct roll gap settings. Input parameters to this system include tundish temperature, roll gap, format, casting speed, water quantities and, most importantly, steel composition specific thermal properties from the DynaPhase model. Fig.2 - DynaGap3D system overview The benefits of accurate steel composition related thermal properties, as an input to the models, cannot be overstated. Earlier versions of software used simple, group related, data whereby a steel grade group would have a range of carbon content. However, the actual steel being cast may have varying carbon, manganese or some other alloying element content. The resultant solid fraction, for example, for the differences in composition, can have a significant effect on the final solidification. In previous projects the influence of soft reduction amount was investigated for bearing steel grades. The carbon segregation index was evaluated for 2 different soft reduction strategies. The soft reduction amount for the first trial was limited to 5 % of the billet dimension. The second cast trial used 7.5 % of the billet dimension as maximum soft reduction, as can be seen in figure 5 on the left picture (red line). The soft reduction amount

CONCLUSION The billet caster at Zenith Steel Ltd. represents a state-of-the-art casting machine for long products. The Connect & Cast automation packages ensure fast ramp-ups with full functionality from the first cast heat. The withdrawal and straightening unit is designed as common frame, consisting of 7 pieces of roller pairs, and therefore promises a short exchange time of the unit from and into the casting machine. All inspections and tests as well as alignment and calibration can be executed at the maintenance stand, thus reducing further the planned downtime of casting machine. The combination of new SRD technology (high pinch roll forces linked with optimized roller pitch) and accurate dynamic soft reduction model guarantees a significant improvement of internal quality. From previous projects it can be derived, that higher soft reduction amount can clearly reduce carbon segregation at the billet center. The hard reduction technology
