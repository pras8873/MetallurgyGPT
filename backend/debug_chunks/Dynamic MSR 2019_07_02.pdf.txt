The metallurgical length depends mainly on steel grade, steel super-heat, casting speed, cast product shape, cast product cross section area, primary and secondary cooling parameters. The metallurgical length is calculated by COOL solidification model and by Level 1 life-time model. Selection of metallurgical length The metallurgical length working value can be selected on the HMI among 3 choices: 1. HMI input (fixed value) 2. Level 1 life-time model 3. COOL solidification model 2.1 Level 1 life-time model The hot strand is divided in slices (each slice length = 100 mm). Every 100 mm of tracked hot material, a new slice record is generated and inserted into a FIFO buffer of slice records. Force: the MSR rolls are force-controlled according to a pattern of force setpoints. 2. Position: the MSR rolls are position-controlled according to a pattern of hot material reduction setpoints. The hot material thickness upstream the MSR area of application is the position reference to calculate the target reduction. The position regulators adjust the required force within pre-defined boundary limits of minimum and maximum applicable force. The MSR enabling is common to force and position modes. If the MSR function is enabled, the MSR setpoint will be applied to the first upstream MSR roll as soon as that roll is closed on the hot material and the head position passed that roll by a certain distance (section- dependent). Then, the MSR setpoint will be applied to the second upstream MSR roll as soon as the (tracked) deformation of the first roll gets to the second roll. So on for the following rolls.
