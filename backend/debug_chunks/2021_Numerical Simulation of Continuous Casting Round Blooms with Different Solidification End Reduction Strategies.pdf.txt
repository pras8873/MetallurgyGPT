ORIGINAL RESEARCH ARTICLE Numerical Simulation of Continuous Casting Round Blooms with Different Solidiﬁcation End Reduction Strategies WENGUANG HU, CHENG JI, and MIAOYONG ZHU To solve the problem of application of the reduction process in the continuous casting of round blooms, double-roll, three-roll and four-roll staggered extrusion schemes were proposed, and three series of three-dimensional thermal-mechanical coupling models were established to analyze the advantages and disadvantages of each reduction scheme. It was found that the double-roll scheme transfers the reduction deformation to the center of the round bloom and closes the internal porosity more eﬃciently. For each squeeze, the double-roll extrusion scheme can easily cause lateral deformation, which may improve the equivalent strain value to the inside of the round bloom more eﬃciently, and there is a concentration phenomenon in the center after several sets of reductions. The three-roll scheme and the four-roll scheme have diﬃculty causing lateral extension, and the reduction is basically consumed by the harder shell deformation, which causes much more longitudinal stretching deformation of the round bloom. With an increase in the solid fraction (fs), the round bloom will accept a larger reduction under the limit of the initiation of internal cracks. At positions with the same

INTRODUCTION DURING the continuous casting of round blooms, defects such as segregation and central porosity often occur in the liquid-solid zone as the molten steel continuously solidiﬁes from the outside to the inside.[1–3] These defects destroy the homogeneity and compactness of the casting round bloom and seriously aﬀect the organizational structure, which greatly reduces the mechanical properties of products and eventually leads to a serious decline in product quality or even scrap. With the continuous development of continuous casting technology, reduction technology, including soft reduction (SR)[4–6] and heavy reduction (HR)[7–10] tech- nologies, has been gradually recognized as the most eﬀective method to solve the central defects of blooms in the continuous casting process. The present authors have carried out a series of studies on the reduction of billets and slabs, including the constitutive model of the ﬂow stress,[11,12] thermal-mechanical behavior,[13] solute transfer and segregation,[14,15]

--- CHUNK 2 ---
risk of crack initiation,[16] center porosity closure,[10,17,18] and equipment design,[19] which have been well practiced in industry.[4,5,20] However, it is quite diﬃcult to apply the reduction process to round blooms because of the special shape of round blooms. At present, a few scholars have per- formed research on the reduction process and behavior of round blooms, and the reduction process has been applied in the partial continuous casting process of round blooms. Voestalpine Stahl Donawitz GmbH carried out production practice on SR technology in the process of round bloom continuous casting.[21] It was found that the application of SR can play a role in reducing the segregation and shrinkage defects of the round bloom, but at the same time, it often leads to massive hot tears in the vicinity of the core zone, and the solidiﬁcation structure restricts applicable SR parame- ters. Yamanaka and Okuda[22] used a series of experi- ments and a ﬁnite element model to study the reduction process of a round bloom with a liquid core using pressheads of diﬀerent shapes. It was determined that the use of a round presshead with a radius similar to the original bloom is recommended for eﬃcient center cavity reduction and

In this work, a model of double-roll, three-roll and four-roll staggered extrusion for round blooms was investigated to explore the deformation of round blooms under diﬀerent extrusion methods. A circular void with a diameter of 3 mm was added to the center of the round bloom to study the closure eﬀects of diﬀerent schemes on the internal porosity. Finally, the round blooms at diﬀerent fs values were subjected to single-pass rolling, and the tensile strain was compared with the critical value for crack initiation to explore the maximum reduction supported by diﬀerent reduction schemes. CONCLUSION Three types of models of double-roll, three-roll and four-roll staggered extrusion schemes are established to explore the reduction eﬀect of the three schemes on round bloom reduction in continuous casting. The calculation results are summarized as follows: 1. Each double-roll reduction can cause voids along the extrusion direction and casting (y)-direction com- pression during reduction but perpendicular to the pressing direction elongation. However, three-roll and four-roller reduction can cause

--- CHUNK 25 ---
void x-direction and z-direction compression and y-direction elonga- tion. After two sets of staggered reduction, the dou- ble-roll scheme compresses the void in three directions, while the three-roll and four-roll schemes elongate the void in the continuous casting direction. The double-roll scheme can more eﬀectively reduce the void volume. 2. The double-roll scheme can eﬃciently transfer the strain to the inside of the round bloom, and the strain is concentrated in the center of the round bloom. However, the three-roll and four-roll schemes show that more strain is consumed by the bloom shell and less strain is transmitted to the inside of the bloom. In the reduction process, the double-roll scheme causes the lateral extension of the round bloom and helps the transfer of the reduction eﬀect to the center of the bloom, while the three-roll scheme and the four-roll scheme cause the longitudinal extension of the round bloom. 3. In the internal crack initiation risk area of the round bloom, compared to the other two schemes, the double-roll scheme will cause a larger tensile strain, which will lead to the generation of internal cracks if it exceeds the critical value. With an increase in fs in the center of

--- CHUNK 26 ---
the round bloom, each reduction scheme will support a larger reduction amount. For the round bloom with a cross-sectional diameter of 350 mm, when 0.5, 1, and 3 mm reduction amounts are applied at fs values of 0.499, 0.602 and 0.731, respectively, the double-roll scheme transmits the largest tensile strain to the inside of the round bloom where fs is equal to 0.731, but it is still less than the critical strain for internal crack initiation.
