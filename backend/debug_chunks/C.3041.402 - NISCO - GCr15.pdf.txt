Formation of porosity Steel grades containing Cr, Mo elements are prone to form shrinkage holes connected to enrichment of Cr, Mo carbides and Mn sulphides at the centre of the bloom. It correspond to a transverse cut of V-seg visible onto the longitudinal samples. Therefore, one possibility is to limit the %Cr content (1.4-1.54) and %C content (0.98-1.02) to the minimum of the spec and check whether the situation is improving. Content of Mo (<0.1%), Mn (0.3-0.34%) and N (<50 ppm) are not an issue. 2. Tundish temperature It is fundamental that T steel, max = T_liq + 30 K => Max tundish temperature shall be <1480 °C. Tech. spech. foresees SH,max = 30+5 = 35K. Ideal super heat shall be 25-30K max. 3. Samples evaluation I below report some meaningful samples with some comments, here resumes: • MSR2 (fs=0.47-0.57)
