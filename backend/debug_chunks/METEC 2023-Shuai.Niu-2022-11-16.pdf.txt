Industrial application of mechanical reduction in bloom continuous casting Shuai. Niu1, Xinghua. Chen1, Stephan. Feldhaus2, Wei. Deng1, Zhiyong. Hu1, 1 Special Steel Business Unit, Nanjing Iron & Steel Co., Ltd., China, 2 SMS CONCAST, Switzerland. Contact data S. Niu, Special Steel Business Unit, Nanjing Iron & Steel Co., Ltd., Jiangbei New Area, 210035 Nanjing, China, niushuai@njsteel.com.cn Abstract The present study focuses on the internal quality improvement of the spring (54SiCr6) and bearing (GCr15) steel blooms by optimizing the mechanical soft and hard reduction process parameters in continuous casting. Solidification status of the blooms with sections of 255 mm x 300 mm and 330 mm x 420 mm were determined by using the verified solidification model that provided by SMS CONCAST. Theoretical effective reduction region was proposed based on the simulated crater end position of the blooms. Intensive industrial trials were conducted to study the effect of the reduction rate on the V-shaped and centerline segregation of the blooms. The results show that obvious improvement of internal quality of the spring and bearing steel blooms of both sections could be obtained by increasing the reduction amount and applying suitable reduction rate in the effective reduction region during casting. Continuous casting, Spring steel, Bearing steel, Central segregation Introduction Defects that inherited from steel bloom in continuous casting process such as shrinkage porosity, central segregation and V shaped segregation would have great influence on the reliability and service life of the subsequent rolling product. It was reported that applying mechanical soft reduction (MSR) in the final stage of solidification at the crater end region would effectively minimize the central segregation and the V shaped segregation especially for the high carbon and large section steel blooms [1-7]. The MSR principle is relying on its efficient compensation of the solidification shrinkage in the final state of solidification and preventing the suction flow to occur [8,9]. The determination accuracy of the position of the mushy region of the bloom and the reduction region and its amount is the critical precondition for the success of the application of MSR process. Regarding the complicated industrial casting conditions, although several researchers had proposed empirical reduction amount/rate and region for different steel grades and bloom thickness, it is still difficult to form the common criteria for the industrial application of MSR [10-14]. Additionally, while the MSR is usually applied in the mushy region of the bloom, the

mechanical heavy reduction (MHR) is applied in the totally solidified region immediately behind the crater end. The reduction amount of single roller in the MHR unit can normally reach to 10~20 mm or more. With this large reduction amount (similar to the rough rolling process) the compactness of the bloom core can be improved during continuous casting. Thus, the extent of the center porosity can be reduced. The 9# bloom caster in NISCO was designed by SMS CONCAST and started up in March 2020. This 4-strand bow-type caster has a casting radius of 12 m and is equipped with eddy-current mold lever monitoring system, mold electromagnetic stirring (M-EMS) and final electromagnetic stirring (F-EMS), mechanical soft and hard reduction (MSR and MHR). The bloom has two sections of 255 mm × 300 mm and 330 mm x 420 mm which were designed to match the subsequent rolling mills. The main product of this caster is special steel such as gear steel, construction machinery steel, non-quenched and tempered steel, spring steel, wire rod steel, bearing steel etc. Moreover, the solidification model provided by SMS CONCAST has a certain accuracy for both medium and high carbon steels which was verified by the developed

“White band” and “Heavy reduction” methods during the commissioning period [15]. The objective of the present paper is to provide some industrial practice data of the optimization of mechanical reduction during continuously casting spring and bearing steel blooms with two sections regarding the improvement of the bloom internal quality. Simulation result The region that with the center solid fraction range from 0.3~0.9 along casting direction of the bloom is generally considered as the effective MSR region [5,13,16]. Apply reduction within this effective reduction region could improve the compensation effectiveness of the shrinkage in the mushy zone during solidification, and subsequently minimize the V shaped and central segregation. Thus, the determination of the position of the reduction zone and solidification crater by applying the simulation model is critical before industrial tests are conducted. After the trials were conducted, longitudinal section (the center section that perpendicular to bloom wideness direction) samples were taken and prepared for hot deep acid etching to reveal the V shaped and central segregation. Conclusion Extensive industrial mechanical reduction trials were successfully carried out and discussed regarding the improvement of the internal quality of the spring and bearing steel blooms with the sections of 255 mm × 300 mm and 330 mm × 420 mm. The simulation results indicate that compare to secondary cooling intensity and casting superheat, casting speed has a much greater influence on the solidification crater. It is evident that the MSR reduction

--- CHUNK 14 ---
rate and effective MSR zone length have great influence on the internal quality of the spring and bearing steel blooms. From the aspect of solidification macrostructure and central carbon segregation homogeneity, the proposed MSR rate for spring steel (54SiCr6) and bearing steel (GCr15) blooms with the section of 255 mm × 300 mm is 1.8 mm/min and 2.1 mm/min, while in the case of 330 mm × 420 mm section, the proposed MSR rate is 1.5 mm/min and 1.9 mm/min.
