Wildhaber ABSTRACT Saarstahl is one of the world’s leading suppliers for long products of unalloyed and alloyed qual- ity and high-grade steels. Traditionally Saarstahl AG is at the forefront of casting technologies for products with the highest quality demands. In order to increase this technological lead and to achieve improvements both with respect to the quality of the products as well as to increase the flexibility and capacity of the production an investment was made in a new 5-strand billet caster, termed S1. The S1 is equipped to cast 1802 mm2 billets with lengths of 6.0 to 13.5 m. 24 months after awarding the contract for building the new caster S1 the first heat was cast in November 2019. The following paper outlines the main features of the caster and presents met- allurgical results. 1. INTRODUCTION Saarstahl’s LD steel plant in Völklingen was established in the early 1980s. It disposes of 3 LD converters with a tapping weight of 180 t each. The hot iron is supplied by ROGESA (Rohei- sengesellschaft Saar

The investment volume including infrastructure measures amounted to 100 mio €. 2. TECHNOLOGY The continuous caster S1 is a 5 strand bow type continuous caster with curved moulds. The conceptual design of the caster S1 is to cast billets 1802 mm2 with the optimal design for the possibility to carry out mechanical soft reduction (MSR). The caster radius is 10.5 m. The caster and discharge area are designed for maximal casting speeds of 2.5 m/min. The metallurgical length of the caster (distance from meniscus to begin torch cutting) is 31.7 m, the mould length is 1000 mm. The secondary cooling length is 9.0 m and consists of 5 inde- pendently regulated cooling zones. Zones 1 to 3 are equipped with water-only spray nozzles while zones 4 and 5 are air-mist cooling. The length of the withdrawal straightening and MSR unit is 9.3 m. Figure 2

shows photographic views of the machine head, moulds and the tundish, the strand guiding and roller table area with torch cutting machines. Figure 2: Photographic views of the caster S1 Caster Head In order to enable sequence casting, the caster S1 has an electrically driven ladle turret with two independently movable rotating arms. The ladle capacity is 180 t. The steel stream from the la- dle is protected by a sealed box flushed with inert gas that is placed over the pouring point of the tundish. The tundish (delta-type) with a nominal capacity of 21 t is preheated in 3 phases during 3 h up to a temperature of 1200 °C. Steel is teemed into the mould through mono-block submerged en- try nozzles (SEN). Hydraulically actuated mono-block stopper rods regulate the flow. The mould level is controlled by a radioactive measuring system (Co60 source). Powder feeding - only granulated powders are used - is automatic via a metering unit. For this purpose each strand is equipped with a container that rests on a load cell. A defined amount of powder is fed into a small hopper and gravimetrically transported into the mould via a squeeze valve. Both the amount of

The mould is equipped with a cartridge system that allows changing the mould tube including the water baffle, water jacket and secondary cooling zone 1 without dismounting the mould electromagnetic stirrer (M-EMS) and the mould frame (Figure 3). The foot roll frame has 2 roller rings with 4 rolls each. 3 Figure 3: Mould assembly Caster S1 is equipped with a hydraulic resonance oscillation (Figure 4). The oscillator system exhibits an oscillation frame guided by a laminated, zero backlash leaf spring system and driven by a hydraulic cylinder, enabling the adjustment of variable oscillation pattern (sinusodial or non-sinusodial) with a maximum stroke height of +/- 10 mm and an oscillation frequency range of 20 to 400 1/min. Moreover, via pressure measurement in the hydraulic cylinder, an online fric- tion force measurement is implemented. Withdrawal Straightening and MSR Unit / Models The withdrawal straightening / mechanical soft reduction unit consists of 6 MSR modules with 2 roll pairs each (Figure 6). Each of total 12 MSR roll pair consists of one lower idle roll with a fixed position and one driven upper roll that can be vertically actuated by a hydraulic cylinder. The hydraulics of the MSR segments allows each upper roll to be pressed onto the strand with a maximum force of 1000 kN and thereby reducing the thickness of the strand. The reduction of

--- CHUNK 7 ---
the strand can be both by so called mechanical soft reduction and mechanical hard reduction. The first method involves reducing the strand while the core is still liquid or mushy, the other method involves reducing the strand immediately after the point of final solidification. The upper roll of each MSR segment is pressed onto the strand either in force or position control mode. The steel grade dependent force- or position set points for each roll are taken from static tables or are dynamically calculated during casting. 2 SGS 1 water 3 4 SGS 2 air mist 5 SGS 3 air mist 5 Figure 6: MSR Unit (Schematic) The deviations are maximum +/- 2 kN in force control and +/- 0.1 mm in position control mode. It is a hollow frame construction in which the rollers, bearings, frames, motors, hoods and cylin- ders are water-cooled by 2 independent circuits. To monitor and regulate the metallurgical working range during MSR the caster is coupled to a dynamic solidification model (DSC, Dynamic Solidification Control). The model calculates the solidification and the temperature distribution of all strands based on the actual casting parame- ters. In case that a deviation occurs between defined set-points and

--- CHUNK 8 ---
the calculated values of metallurgically relevant criteria such as straightening temperature or point of final solidification the casting parameter are autonomously corrected accordingly. The MSR set-points are stored in steel grade specific tables. Depending on the chosen regulation method, these set points can be adhered by using the model to adjust the secondary cooling or the casting speed. It is there- by possible to reduce strand and process specific fluctuations of the MSR working range and increase the reproducibility of the quality values when applying the MSR technology. Roller Table Area The discharge of the billets is fully automatic. Material tracking occurs via light barriers and IR sensors. After exiting the withdrawal straightening unit torch cutters cut the strands into billets with lengths ranging from 6.0 to 13.5 m. The roller table area (Figure 7) is divided into 7 groups, each roll is driven. The steel tape dummy bars are raised on top of the roller table by a hydraulic lifting unit after disconnection from the hot strand in the region of roll groups 2 to 4. At the end of roll group 4 the billets are stopped by retractable end-stops to be marked with heat-, strand-, and billet numbers.

--- CHUNK 9 ---
Two re- volver head billet marking machines are mounted on a bridge crossing the roller table. During normal casting, marking machine no. 1 marks strands 1 to 3, marking machine no. 2 marks strands 4 and 5. Should one of the marking machines be defective all strands can be marked with a single marking machine. After marking, samples can be cut for example for sulphur prints. For this purpose a sample torch cutter is installed on every strand. At roll group 6 one deburrer is installed for each strand. The deburrer consists of two rolls loaded with cutting plates which can be hydraulically raised or lowered above or below the billet pass line independently. Each roller is individually powered by an electric motor assisted by a flywheel. Seen in the casting direction, the first roll deburrs the billet tail in the opposite of casting direction (counterclockwise), the second roll deburrs the billet head in the casting direction (clockwise). A support roller in between supports the billet during the deburring process. The burrs that remain on the head and tail of the billets after torch cutting and sample cutting are thereby removed so that no further conditioning of the billets is

required before rolling. At the end of roll group 7 the billets are marked with the strand number with a marking device that is integrated into fixed endstop. 6 Figure 7: Schematic and photographic views of the roller table Turn Over Cooling Bed Roll groups no. 6 and 7 transport the billets to the endstops of the roller tables. They are pushed out of the roller table with a cross skid, the so-called positioning skid, then pushes the packages to the foot of the approx. 5 m high elevator. From there the billets are discharged via the turn over cooling bed. The turn over cooling bed (TOCB) consists of 4 independent cooling beds and an intermediate roller table between TOCB1 and TOCB2. The total length is approx. 100 m and the width is 12.3 m (Figure 8). The length results from the fact that the billets at the end of the cooling bed may have a maximum temperature of 100°C, since they are loaded directly onto railway wagons. 200 fans are installed below TOCB2, 3 and 4 which can be used to achieve the required 100°C for shorter lengths and thus shorter residence times on the cooling bed. The maximum

total load on the cooling bed is 1.100 t. The billets are placed on the TOCB1 by the elevator. An intermediate roller table is installed be- tween TOCB1 and TOCB2 as the billets have to be transported approx. 9 m against the casting direction. The reason is that the roller tables of the continuous caster S0 need to be crossed via a bridge for approx. 15.2m. At the end of the TOCB4 there is a collection table on which the bil- lets are pushed together in variable pack thicknesses (up to 5 per pack) using a cross skid. The cooling beds are hydraulically driven and consist of lifting cylinders (TOCB1: 8 pieces; TOCB2 to 4: 4 pieces per WKB) and driving cylinders (TOCB1: 4 pieces; TOCB2 to 4: 2 pieces per TOCB). The structure and function is the same for all 4 beds. They are designed as so- called 'floating type', which means that the moving frame is supported directly by the vertical cyl- inders and moved vertically. There are fixed and movable rakes. 7 Figure 8: Overview Turn Over Cooling Bed The process will be described below: The vertical cylinders raise all the movable rakes together and lift the

billets off the fixed rakes. Then the horizontal cylinders move the mobile rakes horizontally in the extraction direction be- fore the mobile rakes are lowered using the vertical cylinders. This places the billets back on the fixed rake. The vertical cylinders pull the mobile rakes back again, bringing them back to their starting position. This process is repeated as many times as the rakes have notches (berths) before the collecting table takes over the billets. During this time, the billets are cooled by con- stantly rotating them (90° per notch) as they are transported through the rakes. Another advantage of this type of TOCB is that there is very good accessibility under the TOCBs, for example for maintenance work or scale disposal. 3. GRADES CAST AND METALLURGICAL RESULTS. Outlook The main focus of the activities on the caster S1 is the conversion of all grades that are cast on the S0 with soft and hard reduction in the 1502 mm2 format to the 1802 mm2 format on the S1. The first results are on a very good level. The quality groups of cold heading grades and free- cutting steels, which are cast without MSR, have already been fully approved for melting in the 1802 mm2 format. Additionally, the largest proportion of spring steel has already been converted to the 1802 mm2 format. Another main focus of attention is the conversion of qualities that were previously melted in the 2402 mm2 format in the bloom and then reblocked to 1502 mm2. These shall be produced in the 1802 mm2 format in order to save the process step of reblocking. 11 The caster is equipped with the latest automation and communication technology.
