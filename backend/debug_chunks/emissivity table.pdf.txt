Mikron Instrument Company, Inc. 16 Thornton Road, Oakland NJ, 07436, USA sales@mikroninst.com www.mikroninst.com TABLE OF EMISSIVITY OF VARIOUS SURFACES Introduction: Emissivity is a modifying factor used in single color thermometry to achieve a correct temperature reading. Emissivity, or radiating efficiency, of most materials is function of surface condition, temperature and wavelength of measurement. In the following table, values for the total emissivity of various surfaces, as well as spectral emissivity at a given temperature, have been tabulated. Total emissivity is defined as the resultant value when the individual emissivity factors are averaged over the total radiation spectrum being utilized. The user may find that for the application a different emissivity setting is required than the one tabulated. This table, however, will provide the best initial setting. A more refined value should be determined experimentally.
