ORIGINAL RESEARCH ARTICLE Reduction Behavior in Large-Sized Round Bloom During Continuous Casting by Numerical Simulation PENG LAN, LIANG LI, YIFAN LU, HAIJIE WANG, HAO GENG, and JIAQUAN ZHANG Solidiﬁcation end reduction is an eﬀective approach to control the central porosity during continuous casting of round bloom, although it is not widely reported. In the present work, a three-dimensional ﬁnite element model has been developed with coupling heat transfer and mechanical deformation for a u690 mm continuously cast round bloom, and veriﬁed by the surface temperature, shrinkage zone width, reduction crack location, and deformed contour shape. It is found that the contact width between roller and the bloom increases with the increase of reduction amount and approximately in a parabolic relationship. To cover the whole range of the shrinkage zone, the total reduction amount should not be smaller than 25 mm as the width of the shrinkage zone is about 120 mm. The bulge width along the horizontal direction during reduction increases as the total reduction amount increases, and the relationship can be ﬁtted by a parabolic equation. The reduction thickness in the shrinkage zone is larger when the reduction amount is higher, and it also follows parabolic relationship. The deformation

--- CHUNK 1 ---
in the shrinkage zone is more obvious when the reduction is conducted before crater end. The reduction eﬃciency for u690 mm round bloom before solidiﬁcation is between 20 and 30 pct, while after solidiﬁcation is roughly between 12 and 20 pct. It increases with the increase of apparent reduction amount, mainly related to the increase of the strain rate. The deformation of the shrinkage zone in u690 mm round bloom with 30 mm apparent reduction in continuous casting and hot rolling has been compared. The equivalent strain in the shrinkage zone of the round bloom in continuous casting is about 0.047 to 0.052, while that in hot rolling is about 0.031 to 0.036, indicating the reduction eﬃciency of the former is about 1.5 times higher than the latter. https://doi.org/10.1007/s11663-024-03246-4  The Minerals, Metals & Materials Society and ASM International 2024 I. INTRODUCTION CONTINUOUS casting is the most popular technol- ogy in steel solidiﬁcation process in plants, attributed to its high eﬃciency, high automation, high yield, and low waste, especially for the large products with a size between 500 and 1000 mm. Now the central quality of square or rectangle bloom is quite excellent due to the well application of solidiﬁcation

--- CHUNK 2 ---
end reduction in continuous casting process.[1,2] It can eﬀectively improve the homogeneity and soundness by the mechanical compensation of volume contraction in the ﬁnal solidiﬁcation stage, preventing the absorption of the enriched liquid or accumulation of the cavity.[3] How- ever, for the large-sized round bloom, the central quality is still a serious challenge during continuous casting, as severe porosity is frequently observed. In fact, the mechanism on the formation of central porosity in continuously cast bloom is quite clear. It usually occurs along the central line of the bloom where the liquid feeding is not active. Mechanical reduction on solidiﬁcation end has been regarded as the most eﬀective method to minimize the size of central porosity during continuous casting.[4,5] However, there are just a few studies focusing on the solidiﬁcation end reduction technology for round bloom. The early application of reduction on round bloom during continuous casting was conducted by Amano and Morii about 30 years ago.[6,7] The center segregation of bearing steel with a diameter of u350 mm product by NO.2 vertical caster was signiﬁcantly improved when the central solidiﬁca- tion fraction of reduction location was 0.4. This technology was also employed to produce 300 and 400 series stainless

the contour as circular as possible.[10] The diameter of the round bloom in the trail was 400 mm and the largest reduction amount was 27 mm. Hu and Ji numerically studied the solidiﬁcation end reduction on a round bloom with a diameter of 350 mm by diﬀerent strategies.[11] It is shown that the double rollers scheme makes the reduction deformation into the center of the bloom more eﬃciently than three rollers and four rollers design, indicating the largest sensitivity to internal crack under the same conditions. In 2019, the trail experiment was carried out by us on a u195 mm round bloom of oil tube steel by two rollers unit. The total reduction amount is about 5 mm and the central porosity in the longitudinal section almost disappeared.[12] It is well accepted that the reduction for round bloom is helpful to improve the central quality during continuous cast- ing. However, the geometry of rectangular bloom and round bloom is quite diﬀerent, leading to the great diﬀerence in the reduction control and eﬀect in contin- uous casting process. In order to make good use of reduction technology for continuously cast round bloom with a large diameter, three questions need to be

--- CHUNK 5 ---
answered before implementation: (1) Whether the advantage in controlling center porosity is taken by reduction compared to hot rolling regarding round bloom? (2) What is the detailed peculiarity in the deformation of the contour and the shrinkage zone during reduction? (3) What is the relationship between reduction parameter and contact width between roller and bloom, and how does it inﬂuence the close of center porosity? These behaviors should be illustrated clearly based on the experimental or modeling results. The online measurement on the surface deformation of the bloom is very diﬃcult due to high temperature, high humidity, and high background noise, while it is also impossible to detect the internal change. Numerical simulation is regarded as the available method to illustrate the essential characteristic in the ‘‘black box’’ of continuous casting. Recently, the modeling investi- gations on the solidiﬁcation behavior including heat,[13,14] ﬂow,[15,16] and mass[17–19] transfer in steel continuous casting have been widely reported, and the as-cast defect including segregation, crack, and shrink- age was well solved. For the deformation behavior, Zappulla and Thomas et al.[20] built up a multiphasic model to describe the hoop stress in the shell of stainless steel, and the contact phenomenon between deformed shell and

--- CHUNK 6 ---
copper mold was revealed. Vakhrushev and Kharicha[21] developed a Norton-Hoﬀ-type viscoplastic model to study the stress evolution in the thin slab mold, and the tension and compression of the solid shell were detected along the mold’s funnel within the correspond- ing straightening and bending zones. Chakraborty and Ganguly[22] made a ﬁnite element model (FEM) to determine the optimal taper in continuous casting billet mold, and it is found that the modiﬁed taper proﬁle was considered to be suitable for maintaining minimum air gap between the billet and the mold in both the corner and oﬀ-corner regions. Zong et al.[23] established a three-dimensional FEM model to learn the formation and propagation of internal cracks induced by soft reduction, and good agreement between the simulation and trial results was obtained. Zhou and Yin et al.[24] compared the reduction eﬀect of convex roll and ﬂat roll in slab soft reduction by numerical modeling, and the deformation feature is coincident with the industrial observation. In a recent work by Jiang et al.[25], the simulation research on the reduction for u140 mm billet was carried out, and results from numerical modeling and physical modeling were generally consistent. It is no doubt that the application of

numerical simulation in continuous casting saves plenty of time and cost during solving quality problems. In this work, a three-dimensional ﬁnite element model coupled heat transfer and mechanical deformation for a u690 mm round bloom was developed, and veriﬁed by the surface temperature, shrinkage zone width, reduc- tion crack location, and deformed contour. The formula to calculate the contact width between roller and bloom was regressively ﬁtted, and the deformation character- istics of surface contour and shrinkage zone of the round bloom were depicted. Finally, the reduction eﬃciency was discussed by comparing with the rectangle bloom in the references. DISCUSSION ON REDUCTION BEHAVIOR A. Contact Behavior The contact behavior between roller and the u690 mm round bloom is diﬀerent from

--- CHUNK 22 ---
the slab or rectangular bloom during reduction process in contin- uous casting. The ﬂat roller contacts with the whole width of the slab or rectangular bloom when reduction is conducted, while the concave roller just contacts with part of the circumference of the round bloom. The contact width between the roller and round bloom depends on the reduction parameters. Figure 10 shows the diﬀerent contact states between NO.2 roller and the u690 mm round bloom with reduction amount of 5 and 40 mm, and the contact widths are about 46 and 150 mm, respectively. It is obvious that the contact width WR increases with the increase of reduction amount, relating to the diameter of the concave part in the roller and the deformation of the round bloom at high temperature. In order to minimize the porosity eﬀectively in the whole shrinkage range during solidiﬁcation, the contact width should cover all the shrinkage zone to provide enough compensation. Thus, the relationship between contact width and reduction parameter at diﬀerent withdraw units should be revealed. VIII. CONCLUSIONS The solidiﬁcation end reduction on a u690 mm continuously cast round bloom was numerically studied, and the deformation behavior was revealed with the comparison to the trail results. The following conclu- sions can be drawn: 1. A 3D FEM model with coupling heat transfer and mechanical deformation during solidiﬁcation in a u690 mm round bloom continuous casting has been developed; the predicted surface temperature, shrinkage zone width, reduction crack location, and deformed contour shape are in good agreement with the measured results. 2. The contact behavior between roller and the u690 mm round bloom is diﬀerent from the slab or rectangular bloom during reduction process in con- tinuous casting. The contact width increases with the increase of reduction amount and in a parabolic

relationship. In the present trial, the width of the shrinkage zone in the present work is about 120 mm, thus the total reduction amount should not be lower than 25 mm. 3. The deformation of the round continuously cast bloom during reduction leads to a near-ellipse con- tour. The bulge width along the horizontal direction increases as the total reduction amount increases. The relationship between bulge width and total reduction amount can be ﬁtted by a parabolic equa- tion when the small diﬀerence between diﬀerent withdraw units is ignored. 4. The reduction thickness in the shrinkage zone is larger when the reduction amount is higher, and it follows parabolic relationship well.
